The Clockmaker

样文 · 三段 · 划哪里都行

For most of his life, my grandfather made a living repairing clocks in a town that time itself seemed to have forgotten. The work was slow and exacting, and it took a toll on his eyesight, but he never once spoke of giving it up. When the factory on the edge of town shut down, and half the families packed up and left, he stayed put — partly out of stubbornness, partly because he had carved out a place for himself that no one else could fill.

Business came in fits and starts. Some winters he barely scraped by; other years, orders poured in from collectors three counties away. Through it all he kept meticulous records in a leather notebook, convinced that sooner or later the details would come in handy. He was, in his own quiet way, playing the long game.

Looking back, I think what set him apart was not skill so much as temperament. He could sit with a problem for hours without losing patience, turning it over until something gave way. In an age that prizes speed above all else, there was something quietly radical about a man who refused to be rushed.
